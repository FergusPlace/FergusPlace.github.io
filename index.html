About Me

A Blurb

As an undergraduate at McGill University I was an active member of the Big Buddies tutoring organization, a volunteer program that focuses on teaching and mentoring underprivileged students in the Montreal area. My time at Big Buddies fostered a great deal of personal growth, specifically in mathematical instruction. I developed techniques and methods to provide clear instruction, alleviating the fears and anxiety associated with math. Beyond instruction, I believe in cultivating an interest in abstract problem solving. While many students may not pursue math after high school there are universal skills that are refined in its learning. My personal areas of study: Analysis, algebra, statistics, probability, calculus, differential equations, discrete mathematics, and set theory. My primary focus is middle school- high school level mathematics and physical sciences.
